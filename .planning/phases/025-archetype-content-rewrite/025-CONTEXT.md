# Phase 2.5: Archetype Content Rewrite - Context

**Gathered:** 2026-02-25
**Status:** Ready for planning

<domain>
## Phase Boundary

Reframe all archetype copy from "you as a parent" to "the parenting you received." The product is The Mirror — it shows users the parenting they received, not the parenting they do. Quiz questions are already past-tense and don't need rewriting. This phase targets: archetype taglines, foundational pattern content, watchout content, cultural overlay content, research anchor sentences, landing page archetype preview copy, and any quiz intro/header text that references "your parenting style."

</domain>

<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Addressee & Voice
- "You" = the adult child who received the parenting, NOT the user as a parent
- Reference caregivers as "the people who raised you" — inclusive of non-traditional caregivers (grandparents, aunts, foster parents), avoids assuming two-parent household
- Primary tense: past — "you grew up with...", "the home you knew had..."
- Taglines MUST be rewritten to match parents-lens (e.g., "You grew up anchored." not "Your calm is your children's foundation.")

### Archetype Names
- Keep all 9 existing names as-is (The Steady Anchor, The Fierce Guardian, etc.) — recontextualize through surrounding copy
- Introduce archetype on result page as: "You were raised by: [Name]"
- Follow with: "The people who raised you parented from a place of [core quality]. Here's what that looked like for you growing up — and what it gave you."
- Landing page archetype preview: reframe copy to match parents-lens
- Quiz intro/headers: audit for "your parenting style" references and fix mismatches only — don't touch question text (already correct past-tense)

### Watchout Framing
- Focus on what the user ABSORBED, not what parents did — no parent blame
- Pattern: "You may have learned that..." + "Many people raised this way find..."
- Watchouts connect past to present: past origin + present echo ("You learned X growing up... today, that can show up as Y")
- Headline pattern: "Growing up with this kind of [quality], you may have absorbed a quiet rule: [internalized belief]. That served you then. It may not serve you now."
- Normalizing language throughout — "many people raised this way find..." not "you probably do..."

### Research Anchors & Citations
- Citations (researcher, work title, year, relevance note): keep exactly as-is
- Research anchor sentences: update framing to parents-lens ("Research on attachment shows that children raised with..." instead of "Research shows that parents who...")

### Cultural Overlay Structure
- Expression modifier: keep factual/observational tone (not personal second-person)
- Strengths bullets: shift to receiver perspective — "what this gave you" (e.g., "The behavioral reliability you grew up with aligned with group harmony — giving you a deep sense of belonging")
- Tensions bullets: shift to receiver perspective — "what this may have cost you" (e.g., "Emotional expressiveness may have felt unfamiliar growing up — leaving you with fewer models for naming what you feel")
- Gently acknowledge where cultural norms reinforced patterns that were hard to see as a child ("In this context, this pattern was often reinforced by the community around you")
- All 45 cultural overlays (9 archetypes x 5 cultures) must be rewritten — no partial rewrite

### Claude's Discretion
- Exact wording of each rewritten tagline (within the "you grew up..." pattern)
- Per-watchout judgment on how much present-day connection to draw
- Per-cultural-overlay judgment on where "norms reinforced this" adds value vs risks offense
- How to handle quiz section headers that may or may not need changes

</decisions>
