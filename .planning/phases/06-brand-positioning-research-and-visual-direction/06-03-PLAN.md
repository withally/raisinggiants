  <name>Task 1: Build the positioning narrative from competitive landscape to brand territory</name>
  <files>.planning/phases/06-brand-positioning-research-and-visual-direction/06-03-positioning-narrative.md</files>
  <action>
Create the positioning narrative document. This REPLACES the existing 06-03-positioning-narrative.md (which pre-committed to "grounded self-discovery").

The document must follow a discovery-first narrative arc: landscape evidence leads to gap identification leads to territory selection. The reader should feel the territory was discovered, not pre-decided.

**Document structure:**

**Section 1: The Millennial Parenting Landscape**
Summarize key findings from the competitive landscape (06-01). Reference specific competitors and the positioning map. Key narrative beats:
- The market is large, crowded, and fragmented across 5 segments
- Products cluster around child-centric needs and parent-behavioral-change
- The parent-centric + self-understanding quadrant is empty
- The cycle-breaking cultural movement proves demand exists for this quadrant

**Section 2: The Gap — What Millennial Parents Need and Nobody Provides**
Articulate the whitespace in the audience's language, not brand-strategy language. What is the unmet emotional need? Frame it from the millennial parent's experience:
- "I want to understand why I parent the way I do — not just how to parent better"
- "I know my childhood affects my parenting, but no product helps me see HOW"
- "Therapy is one tool, but it is clinical, expensive, and positioned around healing — I want revelation, not repair"

**Section 3: Three Candidate Territories**
Evaluate the three territories from RESEARCH.md. For each, provide:
1. One-sentence territory statement
2. How it addresses the gap
3. Pros: why this territory works for a multi-product brand
4. Cons: what risks or limitations exist
5. Multi-product fit: can The Mirror, The Blueprint, AND The Partner Match all live here?

The three territories (from RESEARCH.md):
- Territory 1: "The self-understanding layer for millennial parents"
- Territory 2: "Digital tools for millennial parents who are raising themselves while raising their kids"
- Territory 3: "The parenting brand for the generation that thinks about their own upbringing"

**Section 4: Territory Selection**
Choose one territory (or synthesize from the three) with explicit reasoning. The selection must account for:
- Competitive defensibility (can a competitor enter this territory easily?)
- Multi-product scalability (does it constrain future products?)
- Audience resonance (does it use language millennial parents already use?)
- Brand distinctiveness (does it separate Raising Giants from Good Inside and therapy platforms?)
- Longevity (does it depend on trend vocabulary that may age?)

**Section 5: Brand Architecture — Branded House**
Define the brand architecture using the Branded House model (Apple model) identified in RESEARCH.md. Explain:
- The master brand carries the trust and value system
- Each product solves a distinct problem within the same value system
- Product naming convention: "The [Product] by [Brand]"
- How the brand system accommodates future products beyond the current three

**Section 6: Emotional Core**
Define the emotional core of the brand — the feeling the brand must consistently create. Consider whether "I always sensed this. Now I see it." (from old research) still works as the emotional heart of the new positioning, or whether it needs to evolve. The emotional core must work at the brand level, not just the Mirror product level.

**CRITICAL: The narrative must arrive at the territory through evidence, not assertion.** If the executor reads the competitive landscape and the evidence points somewhere other than the three pre-identified territories, follow the evidence. The user's explicit instruction is: "Let the research reveal the gap."

**Do NOT:**
- Pre-commit to "grounded self-discovery" as the territory name (this is from the old Phase 6)
- Use "cycle-breaking" as brand vocabulary (per RESEARCH.md Pattern 4 — it is entry-point cultural context, not brand voice)
- Build positioning around The Mirror specifically (it must work for all products)

  <action>
Append to the positioning narrative document:

**Section 7: Brand Name Evaluation — "Raising Giants"**

Re-evaluate "Raising Giants" under the multi-product millennial parent brand frame. This is a FRESH evaluation — do NOT carry forward the old Phase 6 conclusion (which was: change to "Imprint") without re-testing.

**Evaluation criteria (all must be assessed):**
1. **Clarity:** Does a millennial parent hearing "Raising Giants" for the first time understand what kind of brand this is? Test against the chosen territory.
2. **Inclusion:** Does the name work for all family structures (single parents, non-traditional families, child-free adults who were raised by someone)?
3. **Direction:** Does the name point toward the brand's value (self-understanding) or away from it (parenting advice for raising great kids)?
4. **Register:** Does the name match the brand's emotional register (revelation, not therapy; warm, not clinical; adult, not child-coded)?
5. **Multi-product fit:** Does the name work as the umbrella for "The Mirror by [Brand]", "The Blueprint by [Brand]", "The Partner Match by [Brand]"?
6. **Misidentification risk:** How likely is a first-time encounter to misidentify this as a parenting-advice brand or a children's product? (The old CONTEXT.md documented this as actually occurring.)

**Consider the reframing hypothesis from RESEARCH.md:** "Raising Giants" could mean "raising yourself to be a giant parent" (self-development framing) rather than "raising children who are giants" (child-outcome framing). Test whether this reframing is findable without explanation or requires interpretive work.

**Alternative name evaluation:** If "Raising Giants" fails the criteria, propose 3-5 alternatives that pass. For each alternative:
- Name
- How it performs on the 6 criteria
- Domain availability indication (note: user must check actual availability; this is directional)
- Risks/tradeoffs

**Product name evaluation:** Assess each product name against the chosen brand territory:
- The Mirror — does it serve a revelation/self-understanding brand?
- The Blueprint — does it serve a multi-product brand beyond self-discovery?
- The Partner Match — does it serve the same brand value system?

**Clear recommendation:** End with a clear keep/change recommendation for the brand name and each product name, with explicit reasoning tied to the chosen positioning territory.
