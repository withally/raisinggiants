  <action>
Create the positioning narrative document. This is the PRIMARY deliverable of Phase 6 per CONTEXT.md. It must answer exactly three questions.

Read the competitive landscape (06-01-competitive-landscape.md) and reference brand gallery (06-02-reference-brand-gallery.md) first. Synthesize their findings — do NOT copy-paste sections.

**Document Structure:**

**Section 1: The Landscape (500-700 words)**
Synthesize the competitive landscape into a narrative — not a list. The reader should come away understanding:
- What categories of products exist for self-reflective adults
- Where the market is crowded and where it is sparse
- What emotional registers are overrepresented (clinical, playful, shallow) and underrepresented (deep + warm)
- Reference the positioning map from Plan 01 but narrate it, don't reproduce it

**Section 2: The Gap (300-500 words)**
Articulate the specific unoccupied territory. Be precise:
- What is the gap? "Emotional depth + clinical credibility + adult self-understanding (not advice)" — expand and justify
- Why does this gap exist? (Because "parenting" products look outward at children, "therapy" products use clinical framing, "personality" products are shallow entertainment)
- Why is this gap valuable? (Because the emotional core — "I always sensed this, now I see it" — is profoundly underserved)
- What would a brand occupying this gap feel like? (Reference the emotional patterns from Plan 02)

**Section 3: The Case for Positioning (500-700 words)**
Make the strategic argument:
- Define the brand territory: "grounded self-discovery" — what does this mean concretely?
- What the brand IS: a mirror, research-grounded, past-lens, language-giving
- What the brand is NOT: therapy, wellness, parenting advice, personality quiz entertainment
- The emotional core: "I always sensed this. Now I see it."
- The audience: self-reflective adults (not just parents) — the unifying trait is the desire to understand one's upbringing
- The product's specific misidentification problem and how positioning resolves it

**CRITICAL:** This document must NOT contain visual direction (colors, fonts, layouts). It defines WHAT to communicate and WHO to communicate it to. HOW to communicate visually is Plan 04's job.

Keep it readable in under 20 minutes. Avoid brand strategy filler ("our mission is...", "our values are...", "brand pillars"). This is a focused argument, not a brand bible.

  <name>Task 2: Brand name and product name evaluation</name>
  <files>.planning/phases/06-brand-positioning-research-and-visual-direction/06-03-positioning-narrative.md</files>
  <action>
Add a "Name Evaluation" section to the positioning narrative document. Per CONTEXT.md, "Raising Giants" is open for evaluation, and product names (The Mirror, The Blueprint, The Partner Match) are also open.

**Brand Name Evaluation:**

Test "Raising Giants" against four criteria (from 06-RESEARCH.md):
1. **Clarity:** Does it communicate self-understanding (not parenting advice)?
2. **Inclusion:** Does it include non-parents?
3. **Direction:** Does it suggest looking inward (past) or outward (future)?
4. **Register:** Does it match the grounded-intimate tone, or does it sound aspirational/corporate?

Then generate and evaluate AT LEAST 3 alternative brand name candidates. For each:
- State the name
- Explain the connotation and emotional register
- Test against the same four criteria
- Note any risks (domain availability concern, SEO implications, cultural misreading)

Include a recommendation (keep/change) with reasoning. If recommending a change, rank the alternatives. If recommending keep, explain what reframing makes the current name work despite its misidentification risk.

**Product Name Evaluation:**

For each product name (The Mirror, The Blueprint, The Partner Match):
- Does the name align with the positioning territory?
- Does it evoke the right metaphor for what the product does?
- Any misidentification risk?
- Keep/change recommendation with reasoning

**Naming Principles:**
State 3-5 naming principles that any name (current or new) should satisfy, derived from the positioning narrative. Example: "The name should point inward, not outward" or "The name should feel warm and intimate, not aspirational or corporate."

End with a clear summary: "Recommended brand name: [X]. Recommended product names: [Y, Z, W]."

Note: Name change recommendations should flag implementation concerns (domain, SEO, metadata) but NOT attempt to resolve them — that's Plan 05's job (rebrand execution plan).
