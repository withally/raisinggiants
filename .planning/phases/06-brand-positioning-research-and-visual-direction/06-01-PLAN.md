  <name>Task 1: Discover and profile 10-15 competitors using audience-first methodology</name>
  <files>.planning/phases/06-brand-positioning-research-and-visual-direction/06-01-competitive-landscape.md</files>
  <action>
Research and profile 10-15 competitors across THREE segments. Use audience-first discovery — find competitors by asking "where does a self-reflective adult who resonates with this product currently spend time?" NOT by searching for parenting apps.

**Segment A: Self-Discovery / Personality Assessment (5-6 competitors)**
Start with the competitors already identified in 06-RESEARCH.md (16Personalities, Enneagram Institute, The Attachment Project, Wondermind) and discover 1-2 more. Look for: Enneagram tools, MBTI platforms, attachment style quizzes, journaling apps with self-insight features.

**Segment B: Wellness / Therapy Platforms (4-5 competitors)**
Start with those in 06-RESEARCH.md (Headspace, Hims & Hers, getrelatio.com, Calm) and discover 1-2 more. Look for: therapy-adjacent content brands, mental wellness platforms, self-help digital products.

**Segment C: Parenting / Family / Self-Understanding (2-3 competitors)**
Start with those in 06-RESEARCH.md (BetterUp, generic parenting quiz apps) and identify 1-2 specific ones. Look for: parenting reflection tools, family dynamics platforms, intergenerational pattern tools.

For EACH competitor, use WebFetch and WebSearch to capture a full-funnel analysis:
1. **Brand identity:** Name, tagline, visual register (warm/clinical/playful/corporate), primary emotion evoked
2. **Visual identity:** Color palette (specific hex if possible), typography (font names), imagery style (illustration/photography/abstract), overall aesthetic
3. **Onboarding flow:** How does the user start? What's the first interaction? Free or paid?
4. **Quiz/assessment UX:** If they have one — question style, length, tone, progress indicators
5. **Result delivery:** How do they deliver the insight? Email, on-page, PDF, dashboard?
6. **Monetization model:** Free, freemium, paid, subscription, upsell? Where does the paywall sit?
7. **Email capture strategy:** When do they ask for email? Before or after result? What's the value exchange?
8. **Emotional response:** What feeling does the brand produce? Trust, curiosity, warmth, distance, superficiality, depth?

For each competitor, also document the GAP — what does this competitor NOT do that Raising Giants does?

Format the document as a structured markdown file with:
- A summary table at the top (competitor name, segment, brand register, key gap)
- Detailed profiles for each competitor in sections
- A "Discovery Methodology" section at the top explaining the audience-first approach used

Do NOT default to category-based discovery. The competitor list must include brands from outside the parenting niche — Enneagram tools, journaling apps, therapy-adjacent content brands — because that's where the actual target audience spends time.

**1. Positioning Map (2x2 matrix)**
Create a text-based 2x2 positioning map using the axes defined in 06-RESEARCH.md:
- X-axis: Clinical/Institutional ←→ Warm/Intimate
- Y-axis: Shallow/Generic ←→ Deep/Specific

Place each profiled competitor on the map with a brief justification for their position. Use an ASCII visualization:

**2. Competitive Gap Summary**
Write a narrative section (300-500 words) that synthesizes the positioning map into a clear gap statement. Answer:
- What territory is occupied by existing competitors?
- What territory is UNOCCUPIED?
- Why is the "deep + warm" quadrant underserved?
- What would a brand need to communicate to claim this space?
- How does Raising Giants' actual product (self-understanding for adults about their upbringing) uniquely fit this gap?

**3. Segment-Level Insights**
For each of the three segments, write a 2-3 sentence insight about what the segment does well and what it misses — informing where Raising Giants can learn from and where it must differentiate.

The gap analysis is the single most important output of this plan — it directly feeds Plan 03 (positioning narrative). Make it specific and defensible, not vague ("there's a gap in the market").
