## Decisions Made

**[06-03-A] Brand name change: Raising Giants → Imprint**
"Raising Giants" fails all four evaluation criteria. "Imprint" passes all four: it communicates something done to the user (not by the user), it is past-lens compatible (an imprint is a completed action), it includes non-parents, and it carries a warm-intimate register (not aspirational or corporate). Implementation concerns (domain, SEO, metadata) are Plan 05 scope.

**[06-03-B] Brand territory: grounded self-discovery**
The competitive gap established in 06-01 and the emotional patterns from 06-02 both point to the same territory: the intersection of clinical credibility and emotional intimacy. "Grounded" carries three simultaneous meanings (research-backed, past-as-foundation, not-abstract). "Self-discovery" specifies the inward orientation. The full phrase is the positioning claim.

**[06-03-C] Emotional core: "I always sensed this. Now I see it."**
This six-word statement is the emotional proposition in its purest form. It contains the distinction from all competitive alternatives — not "I know my type" (validation), not "now I will be better" (improvement), but "I now see what was always true." Every brand decision should be tested against this statement.

**[06-03-D] Audience: self-reflective adults regardless of parenthood**
The unifying trait is the disposition to want to understand how one's upbringing shaped who one is today. Non-parents are the primary growth segment — they are excluded by "Raising Giants" and would be included by a more accurate brand name.

**[06-03-E/F/G] Product name evaluations**
The Mirror: keep (mirror metaphor is load-bearing for the revelation-without-prescription positioning). The Blueprint: keep (correct semantic shift from Mirror to Building). The Partner Match: change when Product 3 is built (Match implies compatibility quiz, not shared introspection).
