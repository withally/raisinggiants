**Section 1: Visual Philosophy — The Synthesis**

Articulate the core visual thesis:

- **The structure:** #5 Neo-Minimalism provides the container. Clean layouts, generous whitespace, one bold typographic moment per section. Type IS the composition. The structural discipline of premium minimalism.
- **The soul:** #6 Emotion-Led provides the human differentiation. Hand-drawn details, organic imperfections, visible craft. In a world where AI can generate pixel-perfect interfaces, visible human craft IS the differentiator. This is not decoration — it's brand DNA.
- **The texture:** #9 Raw Authenticity provides the surface quality. Intentional imperfection. "Someone made this with care, and you can tell." NOT rough or careless — precise, intentional craft that happens to show the human hand.
- **The color:** #7 Vibrant Color reinterpreted within existing palette. Gradient layering (teal-to-deeper-teal), color used as emotional signal not decoration.
- **The edge:** #2 Brutalist as whisper only. Deliberate asymmetry in select layouts. A grid break here, a raw typographic choice there.

**KEY RESOLUTION (explicitly state this):** v3 said "NOT hand-drawn, NOT analog, NOT craft" — positioning Kin as a Calm/Headspace clinical-digital product. The 2026 trend research contradicts this. The resolution: Kin is a **premium digital product that uses visible human craft as its PRIMARY DIFFERENTIATOR from AI-polished sameness.** The warmth comes from BOTH content AND visible craft in the interface. This is not School of Life "loose analog" — it's intentional, precise craft that happens to show the human hand.

**What this IS vs. IS NOT:**
- IS: A beautifully designed book that lives on the web
- IS: Intentional, warm, spacious, with moments that make you slow down
- IS: Premium digital product with visible human craft
- IS NOT: Cold fintech dashboard perfection
- IS NOT: Rough zine chaos
- IS NOT: Clinical SaaS template
- IS NOT: Loose/sketchy analog aesthetic

**Section 3: Typography**

**THE DECISION: PP Pangaia — KEEP and EXPAND.**

Rationale: The old v3 recommended switching to a high-contrast serif (Canela/DM Serif Display) for a "clinical-digital" aesthetic. But in light of #6 Emotion-Led design (humanized letterforms, organic warmth), PP Pangaia is actually MORE aligned with the 2026 trend direction.

PP Pangaia is a geometric-humanist display face with:
- Warmth and personality that high-contrast serifs lack
- Organic letterforms that connect to #6 Emotion-Led
- The Ultralight weight at large scale creates the "type IS the composition" effect from #5 Neo-Minimalism
- The Italic forms feel intimate and personal — emotion-led, not clinical
- Already loaded in the codebase (6 weights: Ultralight, Ultralight Italic, Medium, Medium Italic, Bold, Bold Italic)

A Canela-style serif would push toward the exact "hyper-polished" aesthetic that the 2026 trends are reacting AGAINST. PP Pangaia's geometric-humanist warmth embodies "precision + humanity."

**Type system:**

| Role | Font | Weight | Usage |
|------|------|--------|-------|
| Hero headlines | PP Pangaia | Ultralight (200) | 80-140px. Type IS the composition. One bold moment per section. |
| Section headlines | PP Pangaia | Medium (500) | 32-48px. Clear hierarchy without competing with hero. |
| Emotional emphasis | PP Pangaia | Medium Italic (500i) | Pull quotes, the "I always sensed this" moments. The brand's warmest gesture. |
| Strong accents | PP Pangaia | Bold (700) | Archetype names, key terms. Sparing use. |
| Body text | Geist Sans | Regular (400) | 16-18px, line-height 1.6-1.7. Clean, modern, does the heavy lifting. |
| Labels / UI | Geist Sans | Medium (500) | 12-14px, tracking 0.05-0.1em. Navigation, CTAs, metadata. |

**Type scale (responsive):**
- Hero: `text-6xl sm:text-7xl lg:text-[108px] xl:text-[140px]` — KEEP current scale
- Section heads: `text-3xl sm:text-4xl lg:text-5xl`
- Subheads: `text-xl sm:text-2xl`
- Body: `text-base sm:text-lg`
- Small/labels: `text-xs sm:text-sm`

**Typography rules:**
1. ONE bold typographic moment per section — the rest stays quiet (#5 Neo-Minimalism)
2. PP Pangaia Italic is reserved for emotional intimacy — never for decoration
3. PP Pangaia Ultralight at large scale is the hero gesture — never at body size (illegible)
4. Geist Sans does all structural work (nav, body, UI, CTAs)
5. Line length: max 65-75 characters for readability

**Section 4: Imagery and Craft System**

This is the heart of the v3-to-v4 pivot. v3 said "NOT hand-drawn, NOT analog." v4 says: **visible human craft IS the differentiator.**

**4a. Hand-drawn craft elements (#6 Emotion-Led + #9 Raw Authenticity)**

Hand-drawn SVG elements serve as the visual proof that "someone made this with care." They are NOT illustration in the editorial sense — they are craft details woven into the interface fabric.

Inventory of hand-drawn elements (a `components/ui/hand-drawn.tsx` file already exists with three components):

| Element | Purpose | Where Used |
|---------|---------|------------|
| HandDrawnDivider | Organic wavy line replacing mechanical `border-t` | Between sections, between cards, between content blocks |
| HandDrawnQuoteMark | Organic open-quote for testimonials/pull quotes | KOL quotes, user testimonials, emotional emphasis text |
| HandDrawnCircle | Imperfect circle accent | Eyebrow labels, bullet decorations, section markers |
| HandDrawnUnderline (ADD) | Wavy underline for key phrases | Hero headline word emphasis, section title accents |
| HandDrawnArrow (ADD) | Organic directional arrow | CTA hints, "scroll down" indicators, step connections |
| HandDrawnStar (ADD) | Imperfect asterisk/star | Callout markers, footnote indicators |

**Rules for hand-drawn elements:**
1. **Intentional, not scattered.** Each hand-drawn element has a specific purpose. They mark moments of emotional significance, not every border.
2. **Consistent stroke quality.** All elements share the same stroke weight (1.2-1.5px), same organic curve language, same "precise imperfection."
3. **Color: inherit from context.** Use `currentColor` or the Warm Sand/Burnished Amber accent palette — never pure black strokes.
4. **Density: sparse.** 2-4 hand-drawn elements per viewport. Overuse kills the effect.
5. **SVG, not images.** All hand-drawn elements are inline SVG in React components — scalable, color-customizable, zero network requests.

**4b. Illustration direction**

Brand-level illustration encodes "the parent's interiority — their psychological inner life" (from 06-03). This is NOT the clinical/geometric style from v3. The register is:

- **Intentional craft:** Each illustration looks like it was made by a skilled human, not generated by AI
- **Organic line quality:** Slight imperfections in stroke weight and curve — visible hand
- **Muted palette integration:** Illustrations use the Kin palette colors (Deep Teal, Merlot, Warm Sand) — never full-spectrum
- **Subject matter:** Interior psychological states, not product metaphors. A parent sitting with a thought, not a mirror reflecting
- **Register:** Between School of Life loose-editorial (too rough) and Headspace geometric-precision (too clinical). The sweet spot: refined craft illustration with visible human touch

Name 2-3 reference touchpoints:
1. Lotta Nieminen — clean, intentional, visible craft within restraint
2. Olimpia Zagnoli — bold, human, personality in every line
3. The Kinfolk aesthetic (early issues) — editorial restraint with warmth

**Multi-product illustration test:**
- Brand level: interiority scenes (works everywhere)
- The Mirror: reflection/recognition moments
- The Blueprint: mapping/discovery moments
- The Partner Match: dialogue/connection moments

**4c. Photography (if used)**

Direction for any photographic content:
- Subject: Adults in contemplative, private moments — NOT "happy family stock"
- Style: Warm natural light, shallow depth of field, editorial grain
- Tone: Intimate, observational — as if catching someone in a moment of recognition
- Treatment: Slight desaturation toward the Kin palette, never hyper-saturated

**Section 5: Layout Approach**

**5a. Structural backbone (#5 Neo-Minimalism)**

The layout philosophy: generous space, clear hierarchy, one dominant element per section.

- **Whitespace as design element.** Section padding: `py-28` to `py-36` (up from current `py-20`). The breathing room IS the premium signal.
- **One bold moment per section.** Every section has ONE element that dominates (usually a headline or a key stat). Everything else is subordinate.
- **Max-width variation.** Not every section uses `max-w-6xl`. Hero can go wider; intimate sections can go narrower (`max-w-3xl` for quote/testimonial sections).
- **Full-bleed moments.** Selected sections break the container (teal background sections, hero) for visual contrast and pacing.

**5b. Layout patterns**

| Pattern | Where | Description |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Dramatic left-aligned hero | HeroSection | Headline flush-left with vast whitespace on right (desktop). Type IS the composition. |
| Asymmetric bento grid | KOLCredibility | Break the rigid 2x4 researcher grid into varied-size cards. Some researchers get 2-column span for visual hierarchy. |
| Centered emotional moment | SocialProof / testimonials | Narrow max-width, centered, large italic PP Pangaia. A pause in the scroll. |
| Full-bleed immersion | SecondaryHero | Deep teal background with gradient depth, text centered. An atmospheric shift. |
| Staggered card layout | ArchetypePreview | Cards at slightly different vertical positions. Not grid-perfect (#2 Brutalist whisper). |

**5c. Motion and interaction**

Purposeful, organic motion — never mechanical.

- **Scroll-reveal:** Gentle fade-up (already implemented via `ScrollReveal` component). Use `--ease-organic: cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)` for all transitions. Distance: 20-24px. Duration: 600-800ms.
- **Stagger:** Children within a section reveal with 80-150ms stagger delays.
- **Breathing animation:** Subtle pulse on decorative elements (grain, teal gradient blob). Already implemented as `animate-breathe`.
- **NO parallax.** Too trendy-for-trends-sake. Keep motion restrained and purposeful.
- **`prefers-reduced-motion` respected.** ScrollReveal already handles this. All CSS animations use the media query.

**5d. Texture and surface (#9 Raw Authenticity)**

- **Grain overlay:** Already implemented as `.grain` CSS class (feTurbulence noise at 0.035 opacity). Apply to hero and key sections — NOT every section.
- **Organic gradients:** Replace flat color backgrounds with subtle radial gradients (teal blob in hero, teal depth gradient on SecondaryHero).
- **Soft borders:** Use `border-[#E8E4DF]` (warm neutral) instead of pure gray borders. Consider HandDrawnDivider as replacement for mechanical borders in key moments.

**Section 6: Brand Tone and Voice**

**Tone attributes (5):**

1. **Revelatory** — The brand speaks in recognition, not advice. "You already sense it" not "Here's what you should know." Do: "The patterns have a shape." Don't: "Learn about your parenting patterns."
2. **Warm** — Peer-level intimacy, not clinical distance. Do: "It's the quiet defaults you didn't choose." Don't: "Research indicates that inherited parenting paradigms..."
3. **Precise** — Economy of language. Every word earns its place. Do: "21 questions. No account needed." Don't: "Our comprehensive assessment consists of carefully selected questions..."
4. **Knowing** — The brand has already understood what the user is only beginning to name. Do: "I always sensed this. Now I see it." Don't: "Discover surprising insights about yourself!"
5. **Spacious** — Copy breathes. Short paragraphs. One idea per sentence. The whitespace in the text mirrors the whitespace in the layout.

**Voice register:**
- Clinical [----X] Personal (lean heavily personal)
- Expert [--X--] Peer (middle — expertise without authority)
- Prescriptive [----X] Revelatory (strongly revelatory)
- Serious [--X--] Light (middle — gravitas without heaviness)

**Vocabulary guidance:**
- USE: sense, pattern, shape, name, recognize, inherit, quiet, still, already, finally
- AVOID: discover, unlock, transform, journey, empower, hack, optimize, level-up
- RULE: "cycle-breaking" is cultural context evidence, not brand vocabulary (from 06-RESEARCH)
- RULE: The brand speaks in revelation, not therapy

**Tagline:** "Finally, the words for it." (already implemented in hero and metadata — from [06-04-G])
