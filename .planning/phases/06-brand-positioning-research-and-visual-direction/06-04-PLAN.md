## Part 1: Design Thesis (keep BRIEF — 1 page max)

The formula: **"#5 Neo-Minimalism + #6 Emotion-Led, seasoned with #9 Raw Authenticity"**

State the target feel verbatim:
> "A premium product that clearly had a human behind it. Not the cold perfection of a fintech dashboard, not the rough chaos of a zine. Something closer to a beautifully designed book that happens to live on the web — intentional, warm, spacious, with moments that make you slow down."

State the v3 conflict resolution in ONE paragraph: v3 said "NOT hand-drawn, NOT analog." The 2026 trends say the opposite. Visible human craft IS the differentiator from AI-polished sameness. This is not School of Life rough — it's precise craft that shows the human hand.

State what the CURRENT page is: a SaaS template. Every section follows `centered headline → subtitle → grid → CTA`. The rebuild breaks that.

State the LOCKED foundation (do not rehash — just reference):
- Palette: Cloud White #F5F4F2, Espresso #1A1008, Deep Teal #0D3D3A, Merlot #6B1E2E, Warm Sand #C8B89A, Burnished Amber #C4892A

- Hand-drawn components: HandDrawnDivider, HandDrawnQuoteMark, HandDrawnCircle (in hand-drawn.tsx)
- Utilities: .grain, .animate-breathe, .section-deep, ScrollReveal, --ease-organic

## Part 2: Page Rhythm Map

Before detailing sections, establish the RHYTHM — the emotional pacing of the scroll. This is what the template lacks. Map the 8 sections as an emotional journey:

| # | Section | Background | Density | Dominant Trend | Emotional Beat |
|---|---------|-----------|---------|----------------|----------------|

For example, the rhythm might be: open/dramatic → intimate pause → curiosity → credibility-dense → deep/immersive → practical → emotional close → quiet exit. The key is that ADJACENT SECTIONS MUST FEEL DIFFERENT — in color temperature, density, layout direction, and scale.

---

## Part 3: Section-by-Section Design Specs

For EACH of the 8 sections, write a detailed composition spec covering:

1. **What it replaces** — the current template version (one sentence)
2. **What trend drives the structure** — which of the 5 trends dictates this section's composition
3. **Layout composition** — specific layout description. Not "generous whitespace" — describe the actual spatial arrangement. Left-aligned or centered? How wide? How much empty space and WHERE? What elements are present and how they relate spatially?
4. **Typography** — which PP Pangaia weight/size for headlines. How big, really? What does the body text look like?
5. **Color and texture** — background color/gradient. Where does grain apply? What colors appear?
6. **Hand-drawn elements** — which specific SVG components and WHERE. If new hand-drawn elements are needed, describe them.
7. **Motion** — what reveals, what animates, what stays static
8. **The ONE bold moment** — what dominates this viewport? What makes someone stop scrolling?
9. **What is NOT here** — what elements from the current version get removed or dramatically reduced

**CRITICAL FOR EACH SECTION: Be specific enough that a developer can build it without asking creative questions.**

The sections to spec (in page order):

**1. HERO (HeroSection.tsx)**

The hero must feel like opening a beautifully designed book. #5 Neo-Minimalism at its most extreme: type IS the composition. The current hero has 5 competing elements (eyebrow, headline, subtitle, CTA group, supporting copy, research badge). The rebuilt hero should have dramatically fewer elements at dramatically larger scale. The headline should fill the viewport — not sit politely in a max-w-3xl container.

Consider: What if the hero is JUST the headline + a CTA? What if all the supporting copy, research badges, and eyebrows move elsewhere or disappear? What if the type is so large it becomes the visual landscape of the section?

Think about flush-left vs. centered. Think about the right side of the screen being intentionally empty on desktop. Think about how the radial gradient blob gets replaced with something more intentional (or nothing).

**2. SOCIAL PROOF (SocialProof.tsx)**

The current version is 3 quote cards in a grid — the most generic landing page pattern possible. For a rebuild driven by #6 Emotion-Led: what if this section is ONE quote at a time, at massive typographic scale, with PP Pangaia Italic? A full-viewport emotional moment. Not 3 small cards competing — one human voice filling the space.

Think about the counter (2,847 people) — does it stay? Does it become a typographic moment itself? Does it move to a different section?

**3. ARCHETYPE PREVIEW (ArchetypePreview.tsx)**

The pill cloud is generic. What does this section look like when the archetype names are a typographic composition — different sizes, different opacities, maybe even different orientations? The nine names scattered across the viewport like words on a page that hasn't been organized yet — that IS the reveal. The sample question card is good and should stay, but how does it relate to the typographic landscape?

#5 Neo-Minimalism says one bold moment. What dominates here: the names themselves or the sample question?

**4. KOL CREDIBILITY (KOLCredibility.tsx)**

The current uniform 4-col grid screams template. A #2 Brutalist whisper means: asymmetric bento where card sizes vary DRAMATICALLY. 2 researchers at 2x size, 3 at 1x, 3 at compact. The layout itself communicates hierarchy — Baumrind and Ainsworth visually dominant because their frameworks are foundational. Not equal-size cards with equal-weight content.

The sketch portraits exist and are good — how do they work at different scales? At the 2x size, a portrait can be prominent. At compact, name + year + one line is enough.

**5. KOL NARRATIVE (KOLNarrative.tsx)**

This is the deep-dive section — 4 researchers with full quotes, discoveries, and Mirror connections. The current version is structured but samey (same layout repeated 4 times). What does this look like as a scroll-through reading experience? #6 Emotion-Led says the quotes should feel like encountering wisdom — large, italic, breathing. The Mirror connections are the brand's voice entering the conversation.

Consider: this section on a deep teal background. A shift in atmosphere. You're entering a different space. The quotes in Cloud White PP Pangaia Italic against deep teal. Full-bleed immersion.

**6. FAQ (FAQ.tsx)**

This section works okay. But can it be more intimate? Narrower max-width (max-w-xl). More whitespace between items. The questions in PP Pangaia to make them feel personal rather than utilitarian.

**7. SECONDARY HERO / CLOSING CTA (SecondaryHero.tsx)**

The emotional close. Currently has a 404 image reference. This should be stripped to essentials: one line, one button, maximum impact. Deep teal full-bleed. The typography does all the work. #5 Neo-Minimalism at its purest — nothing here that doesn't need to be here.

**8. FOOTER (Footer.tsx)**

Minimal. The current footer is fine but should match the new energy. Consider: does it live on the deep teal from SecondaryHero (seamless transition) or return to Cloud White?
