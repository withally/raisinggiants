**For each reference brand, document:**
1. Brand name and category
2. Why it is relevant to Raising Giants (specific connection to millennial parent brand opportunity)
3. Visual identity snapshot: colors, typography, imagery style, overall design register
4. Emotional register: what feeling does the brand create? How does it make the audience feel about themselves?
5. What millennial parents specifically love about this brand (evidence from reviews, social presence, cultural discourse)
6. Brand architecture: single product or multi-product? How are products organized under the brand?
7. Transferable pattern: the specific pattern or principle Raising Giants should consider borrowing (be specific — not "good design" but "editorial serif confidence signals intellectual respect for the reader")
8. What NOT to borrow: the specific element that would not serve Raising Giants (e.g., "Goop's luxury-exclusivity register conflicts with Raising Giants' accessibility")

**CRITICAL:** This is a reference gallery for a multi-product millennial parent brand. Every brand evaluation must be filtered through: "does this pattern work for a brand that houses The Mirror, The Blueprint, AND The Partner Match?" **Do NOT:**
- Include brands only because they have nice self-discovery aesthetics (the old scope)
- Include child-focused brands (Moshi, Headspace for Kids) — these serve the wrong visual register for an adult-facing brand

<task type="auto">
  <name>Task 2: Synthesize patterns across reference brands</name>
  <files>.planning/phases/06-brand-positioning-research-and-visual-direction/06-02-reference-brand-gallery.md</files>
  <action>
Append to the reference brand gallery document (after individual profiles):

**Section: Pattern Synthesis**
Analyze across all profiled reference brands and extract recurring patterns in 5 categories:

1. **Color patterns:** What palette families recur across trusted millennial parent brands? What colors do they avoid? Is there a dominant register (warm earthy, cool clinical, bright playful)?

2. **Typography patterns:** Serif vs. sans-serif prevalence? Display font treatment? What does the typography signal about the brand's relationship to the reader? (e.g., "editorial serif = intellectual respect" vs. "geometric sans = tech efficiency")

3. **Imagery/illustration patterns:** Photography vs. illustration prevalence? What visual metaphors work? What is the subject of imagery (people, abstract, product)? Warm vs. clinical register?

4. **Tone and voice patterns:** How do these brands speak to the audience? Peer-level or expert-down? Personal or institutional? What vocabulary do they use/avoid?

5. **Brand architecture patterns:** How do multi-product brands within the gallery handle product naming, visual hierarchy between brand and product, and coherence across a portfolio?

**Section: Anti-Pattern Synthesis**
Extract 3-5 patterns to explicitly AVOID — things that reference brands do that would harm Raising Giants' positioning. Examples:
- Child-coded visuals (bright primary colors, playful illustration) that signal "product for kids"
- KOL-anchored brand building (Dr. Becky = Good Inside) that creates fragility
- Wellness-overreach aesthetics (Goop) that feel exclusive rather than accessible

**Section: Design Direction Signals**
Distill the synthesis into 3-5 actionable signals for the visual direction brief (Plan 06-04). Each signal should be a specific, implementable direction, not a vague aspiration. Format: "[Signal name]: [What it means] — Evidence: [which reference brands demonstrate this]"
