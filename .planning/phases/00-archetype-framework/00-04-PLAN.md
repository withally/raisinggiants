Update `lib/archetypes/archetypes.ts` to fill in the previously empty `foundationalPatterns` and `watchouts` fields for every archetype.

**For each archetype's `foundationalPatterns` (ARCH-03):**

1. `headline` — A single-sentence positive anchor. Example: "Your calm, consistent presence gives your children a deep sense of safety they carry into the world."
   - Must be strength-first per user decision
   - Must reference the current parenting style, not the childhood origin
   - Must feel like something a parent would want to read aloud to a friend

2. `themes` — 3-5 key theme bullet points. Each theme is a 1-2 sentence structured outline point. Example themes for a "Steady Anchor" archetype:
   - "Emotional predictability — children always know what to expect from you, which builds secure attachment"
   - "Boundaries as love — your structure communicates care, not control"
   - "Calm under pressure — you model emotional regulation that children internalize"
   These are outlines, not full prose. They are the skeleton that Phase 5 AI will flesh out with personalized detail.

3. `researchAnchor` — One sentence linking to the KOL framework that most directly supports this pattern. Example: "Rooted in Baumrind's authoritative parenting style and Siegel's concept of 'being present' as the foundation of secure attachment."

4. `citations` — 1-2 Citation objects referencing the specific researchers/works. Use the KOL synthesis from Plan 01 as the source.

**For each archetype's `watchouts` (ARCH-04):**

1. `headline` — A single-sentence normalized framing. Example: "When stress runs high, your steadiness can tip into emotional distance — and the people closest to you may feel it first."
   - Per user decision: blend of research framing and gentle nudges
   - Tone: "research shows..." + "when stressed, you might find yourself..."
   - Normalizing, NOT pathologizing. Every parent has watchouts.

2. `themes` — 3-5 watchout theme bullet points. Each is a 1-2 sentence structured outline. Example watchouts for a "Steady Anchor":
   - "Emotional suppression risk — research shows that high self-regulation can mask avoidance of difficult emotions (Gottman's 'emotion-dismissing' pattern)"
   - "Under-responsiveness to bids — when stressed, you might miss your child's emotional bids because you're focused on maintaining calm"
   - "Rigidity under pressure — your consistency is a strength, but it can become inflexibility when the situation calls for adaptation"
   Each watchout should reference specific research where possible. The tone should feel like a wise friend pointing something out, not a diagnostic.

3. `researchAnchor` — One sentence linking to the shadow/risk research. Example: "Drawing from Gottman's research on emotion dismissing and Siegel's concept of rigidity as one end of the 'river of integration.'"

4. `citations` — 1-2 Citation objects.

**Quality check for every archetype:**
- [ ] foundationalPatterns headline is strength-first and parent-proud-to-share
- [ ] foundationalPatterns themes are 3-5 specific, not generic
- [ ] watchouts headline uses the "when stressed..." framing, not deficit labeling
- [ ] watchouts themes reference specific research, not vague "studies show"
- [ ] No archetype's watchouts make it sound like a "bad" archetype
- [ ] The pair (patterns + watchouts) tells a coherent story: "this is your superpower, AND here's what to watch for"

  <name>Task 2: Create cultural context overlays for every archetype</name>
  <files>lib/archetypes/cultural-overlays.ts</files>
  <action>
Create `lib/archetypes/cultural-overlays.ts` defining cultural overlays for every archetype across 4-5 named cultural contexts.

Import `CulturalOverlay` and `ArchetypeId` from `./types.ts`.

**Cultural contexts to cover (per research recommendation):**

1. **East Asian collectivist** (Chinese, Korean, Japanese) — emphasis on filial piety, academic achievement, emotional restraint as respect, group harmony
2. **South Asian joint-family** (Indian, Pakistani, Bangladeshi) — intergenerational household, diffuse parenting authority, marriage-family honor dynamics
3. **Latin American familismo** (Mexican, Colombian, Brazilian, etc.) — family centrality, respeto, simpatia, strong maternal identity
4. **Sub-Saharan African community** (Nigerian, Kenyan, Ghanaian, etc.) — communal child-rearing, respect for elders, spiritual/religious integration
5. **Western individualist baseline** (default) — standard archetype description applies; included for completeness but `strengthsInContext` and `tensionsInContext` can be minimal

**For each archetype x cultural context combination, define:**

1. `culturalContext` — the named context (exactly matching one of the 5 above)
2. `expressionModifier` — 2-3 sentences describing how this archetype's core traits MANIFEST DIFFERENTLY in this cultural context. Per RESEARCH.md anti-pattern: overlays modify EXPRESSION, not which archetype a person is. Same archetype, different expression.
3. `strengthsInContext` — 2-3 bullet points: strengths of this archetype that are amplified or particularly valued in this cultural context
4. `tensionsInContext` — 2-3 bullet points: watchouts that are more pronounced or take a different form in this cultural context

**CRITICAL anti-patterns to avoid:**
- Do NOT create stereotypical caricatures. Within-culture variance exceeds between-culture variance (from research). Frame overlays as "tendencies in contexts where..." not "all Chinese parents..."
- Do NOT make any cultural context sound worse than others. Every context has both amplified strengths and amplified tensions.
- Do NOT treat Western individualist as the "normal" baseline and others as deviations. Each context has its own coherent logic.
- Overlays are MODIFIERS, not alternative archetypes. The archetype identity stays the same.

**Data structure:**

Export `CULTURAL_OVERLAYS` as a `Record<ArchetypeId, CulturalOverlay[]>` — keyed by archetype ID, each containing an array of 4-5 CulturalOverlay objects.

**After creating the overlays file**, update `lib/archetypes/archetypes.ts` to import `CULTURAL_OVERLAYS` and assign the correct overlays to each archetype's `culturalOverlays` field (replacing the empty arrays from Plan 02).

**Total content volume:** With ~7 archetypes x 5 cultural contexts = ~35 overlay entries. Each entry has expressionModifier (2-3 sentences) + 2-3 strengths + 2-3 tensions. This is substantial but structured — outlines, not prose.
